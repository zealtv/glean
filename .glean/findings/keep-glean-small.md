# Keep glean small and few

Findings stay small and few. Prefer associating or revising over creating; drop material that does not earn its place.

## Why

A heap of findings is just another inbox. The value of memory comes from the discipline of distillation — not the volume of capture. A small, legible, revisable surface is what makes glean useful at all.

## Triggers

distillation posture, when to create a finding, when to drop, growing tray, too many findings, tray cleanup, prefer association, prefer revision, do not force synthesis, do not mistake compression for understanding

## Context

The preferred order, when working material from `in/`:

1. **Associate** with an existing finding.
2. **Revise** an existing finding if that is enough.
3. **Create** a new finding only when needed.
4. **Drop** the item if it doesn't merit carry-forward.

A finding earns its place by improving judgment. If it doesn't, drop it with a reason.

This finding seeds the system. It encodes the spirit of the protocol so that a fresh glean knows, on day one, what kind of memory it is meant to be.
